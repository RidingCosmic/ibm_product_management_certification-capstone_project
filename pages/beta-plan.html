Beta Plan

Solar-R-Us (SRU) LLC — Commercial Solar Water Heating System Beta Test. Originally written to fit on one page. Same content, laid out here as a field reference.

10 fields Develop Phase 8 testers
In my own words I kept this one tight on purpose, originally it had to fit on a single printed page. Every line earns its place here.
Testing Purpose
Validate that commercial solar water heating system configurations (small, moderate, large) and the companion app meet functional, usability, and market-positioning requirements before commercial launch. Confirm system functions, pricing/value perception, and demo readiness ahead of the planned go-live.
Roles & Responsibilities
Product Manager: leads testing, coordinates schedule, owns outcomes.
UX Team: observes app usability, download/setup time, error self-correction.
Marketing: validates value propositions, positioning, pricing, funds testing ($10,000).
Manufacturing/Operations & Customer Support: observers, provide subject matter expertise.
Participants & Incentives
3 small-system users, 3 moderate-system users, 2 large-system users (8 total), drawn from SRU's existing commercial partner base near the SRU plant. Incentives: SRU pays participant travel costs, provides a catered lunch each test day, and offers a significant discount to upgrade their current system to the new product line.
Tester Contact Method
Participants are sourced through SRU's existing solid relationships with commercial partners already using SRU solar systems. Weekly status meetings and weekly communications keep all participants informed and aligned through the test cycle.
Testing Objectives
(1) Validate all system functions/features work as planned. (2) Validate marketing value propositions, positioning, and pricing options. (3) Confirm planned demo methods are effective. (4) Gain customer feedback and insights. (5) Complete refinements before launch.

UX targets: app download-to-operation in ≤15 minutes; 100% user self-correction on operational errors; 4.5/5 overall satisfaction rating.
Test Venue
On-site at the SRU testing location, near the SRU plant. Prototype systems are configured for small, moderate, and large commercial users on consecutive days, each within a 4-hour testing window (10:00 AM–12:00 PM core testing, 1-hour catered lunch, 1:00–3:00 PM advanced testing).
Communication Methods
Weekly status meetings with all participants and internal stakeholders. Weekly written updates to share status. Post-test retrospective session to capture tester satisfaction, likes, and improvement recommendations.
Costs & Funding
Total beta testing cost: approximately $10,000. Funding source/approval: funded and sourced by the Marketing Director; approval secured prior to test execution. Covers participant travel, catering, and test logistics.
Testing Schedule
Prototype configuration and testing run on consecutive days by system size (small, moderate, large). Final results compiled and analyzed within 1 week of test conclusion. All required system refinements completed within 2 weeks of test conclusion. Launch is delayed only if major refinements are required (none currently assumed).
Legal Considerations
All testers sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) prohibiting release of any system information to the public prior to launch, protecting SRU's competitive positioning and unannounced product details.

8 commercial testers total, sourced from SRU's existing partner base
