Demand Generation Plan

Solar-R-Us (SRU) LLC — Commercial Solar Water Heating System Launch. A six-month campaign to drive demand ahead of launch.

9 sections Launch Phase $280K budget
In my own words This was the most fun document to write, honestly, because it's where the product stops being a spreadsheet and starts being something real people see and respond to.
1

Marketing Objective

  • Drive awareness and qualified demand for the new commercial solar water heating system ahead of the planned launch.
  • Double the number of existing and new commercial firms contacted, from roughly 200 existing customers to over 500 contacted during the six-month campaign.
  • Support the broader business goal of growing SRU's overall market share by 20%.
2

Current Solar Water Heating Market Trends

  • The global solar water heater market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 8.5% from 2023 to 2032, expanding from $4.01 billion to $7.71 billion.
  • Demand is currently outpacing supply industry-wide, creating both an opportunity (strong buyer appetite) and a constraint (installation wait times).
  • Government mandates on emissions and rising energy costs are pushing both commercial and government buyers toward solar alternatives.
  • Buyers are increasingly cost-conscious and actively comparing total cost of ownership, making transparent ROI data a competitive necessity.
3

Marketing Theme

"Save money and the environment with solar water heating."

  • Selected for being simple, accurate, brief, and concise while capturing the core opportunity SRU's systems provide.
  • Not positioned as a novel theme; intentionally straightforward to maximize clarity across a broad commercial audience.
4

Primary Target Market Segment

  • Commercial customers — small, moderate, and large businesses seeking to reduce overall energy costs and maximize profit margins.
  • Includes SRU's existing base of 200+ commercial users with past registration data, plus a researched list of 500+ new potential customers.
  • Residential customers and government facilities are secondary segments, addressed in later launch phases.
5

Promotion Vehicles and Collateral Needs

Digital

  • Website update targeting 1,500+ visits in six months
  • Paid SEO support through an external partner
  • Product demo videos shared on social media

Print & In-Person

  • 10,000 copies each of brochures, sell sheets, fact sheets
  • Trade show mock-up, developed shortly after launch
  • System prototypes for demo at the company site
  • ROI calculator for live demos
  • Competitive comparison white paper within two months post-launch
6

Campaign Schedule / Timeline

Campaign duration

Six months, launching alongside the planned May launch date, optimized to drive sales during summer installation months.

Budget & team

$280,000 supported by a dedicated team of 12 marketing and sales personnel.

Month 2 post-launch

Competitive comparison white paper completed.

Ongoing

Initial system cost-validation testing to confirm and support marketing claims with real data.
